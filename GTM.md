# GTM — Go-to-Market Plan

## Target user

**Engineering managers and DevOps leads at Series A–B startups (20–100 people) who just got an "optimize spend" mandate from their CFO.**

Specifically: the person who owns the AI tooling budget line item, has 5–30 developer seats across 2–4 AI tools, and was just told to "cut 15% from non-headcount spend this quarter." They're not a finance person — they're technical, they know what Cursor and Copilot do, but they don't have time to audit 8 vendor pricing pages.

Secondary: Solo founders and freelancers who suspect they're overpaying but don't know where. Lower LTV but higher volume — they're the sharing engine.

## What they Google right before they need this

- "cursor vs copilot pricing 2026"
- "is claude team worth it for 3 people"
- "chatgpt pro vs plus difference"
- "ai tools cost optimization startup"
- "how much should a startup spend on AI tools per developer"

They're in comparison/evaluation mode, not purchase mode. They want a calculator, not a sales pitch.

## Where they hang out

- **r/ExperiencedDevs** and **r/SideProject** — not r/programming (too noisy)
- **Hacker News** — specifically the "Show HN" and "Ask HN: what AI tools does your team use?" threads (these pop up monthly)
- **X/Twitter lists:** #buildinpublic, DevTools Twitter, indie hacker accounts (Levels, Danny Postma, Marc Lou)
- **Slack groups:** Rands Leadership Slack (#engineering-management), Lenny's Newsletter Slack
- **Discord:** Cursor Discord, Windsurf Discord (users actively discuss pricing there)
- **LinkedIn:** "Head of Engineering" and "VP Engineering" who post about developer productivity

## First 100 users in 30 days — $0 budget

**Week 1: Seed content**
- Post a "Show HN: I built a free audit for AI tool overspend" — frame it as "I was paying $470/mo for my 3-person team and got it to $140 without losing anything, so I built a tool to check your stack too"
- Share the same story on r/ExperiencedDevs with actual numbers (not a link-drop — a genuine "here's what I found" post with the tool link in a comment)
- Tweet a screenshot of the results page showing a real audit with numbers. The shareable URL is the viral mechanic.

**Week 2: Community engagement**
- Answer every "Cursor vs Copilot" and "is Claude Team worth it" thread on Reddit/HN with a genuine 3-sentence answer + "I built a calculator for this: [link]"
- Post in Cursor Discord and Windsurf Discord (these communities actively discuss pricing)
- DM 10 #buildinpublic founders on X who've tweeted about their AI tool stack — "saw you're on Cursor Business + Claude Team, ran your numbers through SpendLens, looks like you could save $X — here's the link"

**Week 3: Amplification**
- Reach out to 3 DevTools newsletter authors (TLDR, ByteByteGo, Pointer) with a one-liner pitch: "Free tool that shows devs where they're overpaying on AI — fits your readers"
- Post a "my AI tool bill went from $X to $Y" thread on X with the SpendLens shareable link as proof
- Cross-post the HN post to relevant Slack groups (Rands, Lenny's)

**Week 4: Compound**
- Every user who shares their audit URL generates a new organic touchpoint (OG preview shows savings amount — that's the hook)
- Follow up with high-savings leads via Credex consultation offer
- Write a blog post: "The 5 most common AI tool overspend patterns I found auditing 50 startups" (using anonymized aggregate data)

## The unfair distribution channel

**The shareable URL IS the distribution.** Every audit generates a unique URL with an Open Graph preview that says "$X,XXX/year in AI tool savings found." When an eng manager shares that in their company Slack or tweets it, every developer who sees it thinks "wait, am I overpaying too?" and clicks through to run their own audit.

Credex's unfair angle: they already have relationships with companies buying API credits. Every existing Credex customer can be sent their own SpendLens audit as a value-add touchpoint — "hey, we checked your full AI stack beyond just API credits, here's what else you could save." That's a warm intro to SpendLens that no competitor can replicate.

## Week-1 traction if this works

- 200+ unique visitors from HN (typical Show HN in developer tools)
- 50+ completed audits (25% conversion from visit → submit)
- 15+ email captures (30% of completed audits)
- 3–5 shareable URLs posted publicly (each generating 10–30 more visits)
- 2–3 Credex consultation leads from high-savings cases

The leading indicator is the share rate. If people share their audit URL, it works. If they don't, the results page isn't compelling enough and needs redesign.
