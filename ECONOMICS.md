# Economics

If Credex deployed SpendLens tomorrow, here's the unit economics breakdown.

## What's a converted lead worth?

**Assumption:** The average qualified lead (>$500/mo in identified savings) converts at 8% to a Credex customer with median first purchase of $2,500.

- Lead value (LTV): $2,500 × 8% = **$200 per qualified lead**
- Credex gross margin on credits: ~18-25% (industry standard for resale arbitrage)
- Actual profit per converted lead: $200 × 20% = **$40**

*Conservative estimate: assumes zero repeat business. In reality, startups return every quarter for renewal, making true LTV closer to $600-800.*

## CAC by channel (from GTM.md)

| Channel | CAC | Conversion % | Qualified leads needed for 1 customer |
|---------|-----|--------------|--------------------------------------|
| Direct HN/Reddit posts | $0 | 2-4% | 25-50 |
| Targeted DMs to founders | $0 (time cost) | 8-12% | 8-12 |
| Twitter share loops | $0 | 1-2% | 50-100 |
| Indie hacker communities | $0 | 5-8% | 12-20 |

**Blended CAC: $0-15** (mostly time/effort, near-zero cash cost)

## Conversion funnel math

From 1,000 audits completed:

1. **1,000 audits** → land on results page
2. **420 (42%)** → show >$100/mo savings (not already optimal)
3. **180 (18%)** → show >$500/mo savings (Credex-eligible, CTA surfaced)
4. **54 (5.4%)** → capture email on LeadCapture form
5. **14 (1.4%)** → book consultation (Calendly click-through)
6. **3-4 (0.3-0.4%)** → actually purchase credits

**Economics per 1,000 audits:**
- Qualified leads: 54
- Consultations booked: 14  
- Customers: 3.5
- Revenue: 3.5 × $2,500 = **$8,750**
- Profit (20% margin): **$1,750**
- Cost to acquire 1,000 audits: **~$0** (organic channels)
- **Net profit: $1,750 per 1,000 audits**

## What makes this profitable

The model only works if:

1. **High organic traffic:** SEO + virality drive CAC to near-zero
2. **Qualified leads only:** CTA only shown when savings >$500/mo
3. **Instant value first:** Email captured *after* value shown, never before
4. **Tool actually helps:** If summaries are wrong, trust collapses and conversion craters

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

| Metric | Month 6 | Month 12 | Month 18 |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Monthly audits | 2,500 | 15,000 | 50,000 |
| Qualified leads/mo | 135 | 810 | 2,700 |
| Customers/mo | 9 | 53 | 175 |
| MRR from new customers | $22,500 | $132,500 | $437,500 |
| Cumulative ARR | $135k | $795k | **$1.05M** |

**What would have to be true:**

- Audit→lead conversion stays at 5.4% (current model)
- Product Hunt launch in Month 2 drives 8k+ audits in first week
- Organic growth compounds at 25%/month through viral sharing
- Credex closes 30% of consultations (vs 25% assumed above) — achievable with founder-led sales
- Retention: customers return quarterly for renewals (not modeled above; adds ~60% to LTV)
- No paid acquisition needed until Month 9+

**The bottleneck is top-of-funnel volume.** If we can drive 50k audits/month organically by Month 18, the math works. If not, we'd need $15-30k/month in paid ads to hit target, which changes unit economics and requires raising a round or taking Credex commission-based.

## Where this model breaks

1. **Competition:** If 3 other tools launch in same space, organic traffic splits 4 ways → need paid acquisition
2. **Pricing transparency:** If vendors publish better self-serve calculators, founders don't need us
3. **Credex supply:** If Credex can't source enough discounted credits to fulfill demand, LTV collapses
4. **Founder fatigue:** "Another SaaS cost optimizer" — if founders ignore the category, no amount of funnel polish helps

The bet: there is no "Mint for AI spend" today, and the next 18 months are a land-grab window before incumbents (Ramp, Brex) add this as a free feature.
